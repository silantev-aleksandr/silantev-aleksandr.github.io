Why this research exists

Control-loop pathology

Broken or absent feedback loops let tail latency & spend variance drift. You can’t buy your way out once regressions compound.

Signals: oscillations, bursty p95/p99 tails, drift after deploys, chronic throttle toggling.

Scientific approach

We identify controllable surfaces, add guardrails, and tune closed-loop control with production telemetry, not slideware.

Outcome: repeatable, debuggable improvements with receipts.

Economic lens

Performance is budget. Stabilizing tails unlocks predictable cost envelopes and faster, safer releases.

We target p95, canary pass rates, and infra spend volatility.

Method & rigor

Identification → control

Map actuators (knobs) to observed outputs (tails & spend). Start with canary lanes, add guardrails, then tune controller gains.

  • Diagnostics: oscillations, saturation, dead-bands
  • Guardrails: bounds, hysteresis, rate limits
  • Receipts: diffs, SLO deltas, rollout envelopes

Stability & optimization

Safeguard stability before chasing optimality. The right order reduces incidents and preserves credibility.

  • Lyapunov-style thinking for safety margins
  • Data-driven, vendor-neutral recommendations
  • No invasive rewrites required
