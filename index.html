Why this research exists

Control-loop pathology

Tails inflate when orchestration reacts faster than the plant can settle: async backlogs, shared-seed backoff, bursty concurrency caps, cache TTL flapping.

Scientific approach

Open-loop identification → discrete controllers → proof-of-stability intuition. Armijo line-search for safe step sizing, Lyapunov-style invariants for guardrails.

Economic lens

We treat cost uncertainty as a risk distribution and control the tail via CVaR-aware governors (token budgets & concurrency), not just averages.

Goal: reduce p95/p99 and unit-economics variance without model or vendor changes — by fixing the loop around the model.

Method & rigor

Identification → control

We characterize delay, loop gain, saturation, and noise. Then we synthesize anti-windup PI for queue/inflight, Smith predictors for long delays, Kalman + CUSUM for noisy KPIs/drift, and ramp/MPC-lite governors for tokens & concurrency.

Stability & optimization

Lyapunov-style invariants act as safety rails. Armijo backtracking selects safe step sizes for tuning. CVaR guards bound the economic tail risk while canary tests (5–20% hash) and non-parametric tail stats prove the win.
